# Design Direction

Picks a cohesive visual style that aligns with brand, audience, and goals.

## Quick Reference

| Industry | Typical Direction | Key Elements |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| Healthcare/Therapy | Calm, trustworthy | Soft colors, rounded fonts, breathing room |
| Tech/SaaS | Modern, clean | Gradients, geometric, bold typography |
| Luxury/Premium | Elegant, minimal | Lots of white space, serif fonts, muted tones |
| Creative/Agency | Bold, expressive | Strong colors, experimental layouts |
| Local Business | Friendly, approachable | Warm colors, photos of real people |

## Trigger Conditions

Use this skill when:

- Starting visual design phase
- Client says "make it modern" (undefined)
- Design feels inconsistent
- Rebrand or refresh needed

## Procedure

### 1. Gather Design Inputs

```markdown
## Design Input Checklist

**Brand Assets:**
- [ ] Logo (and variations)
- [ ] Existing brand colors
- [ ] Current fonts (if any)
- [ ] Photography style

**Audience Expectations:**
- [ ] What do competitors look like?
- [ ] What does the audience trust?
- [ ] What would feel "off" or wrong?

**Client Preferences:**
- [ ] Sites they like (reference URLs)
- [ ] Sites they dislike
- [ ] Words to describe desired feel
- [ ] Any hard constraints
```text

### 2. Define Design Direction

```markdown
## Design Direction

**Overall Feel:** [3-5 adjectives]
Example: Calm, professional, warm, approachable, trustworthy

**Visual Approach:**
- [Minimal / Rich]
- [Light / Dark]
- [Soft / Bold]
- [Organic / Geometric]

**Reference Sites:**
1. [URL] - Like: [what], Avoid: [what]
2. [URL] - Like: [what], Avoid: [what]

**Mood Board Keywords:**
- [keyword 1]
- [keyword 2]
- [keyword 3]
```text

### 3. Create Design Tokens

```markdown
## Design System Tokens

**Typography:**
- Headings: [Font family]
- Body: [Font family]
- Feel: [Rounded / Sharp / Classic]

**Colors:**
- Primary: [hex] - [usage]
- Secondary: [hex] - [usage]
- Accent: [hex] - [usage]
- Neutrals: [light to dark range]

**Spacing:**
- Base unit: [8px recommended]
- Section padding: [multiple of base]

**Borders:**
- Radius: [0px / 4px / 8px / 16px / full]
- Style: [sharp / slightly rounded / very rounded]

**Shadows:**
- Style: [none / subtle / pronounced]
```text

## Direction Archetypes

### 1. Calm & Trustworthy (Healthcare, Finance, Therapy)

```text
Colors: Soft blues, greens, warm neutrals
Typography: Rounded sans-serif or humanist fonts
Spacing: Generous, breathing room
Images: Real people, natural light, candid
Borders: Rounded (8-16px)
Overall: Nothing jarring, quiet confidence
```text

### 2. Modern & Clean (SaaS, Tech)

```text
Colors: Primary blue/purple, vibrant accents
Typography: Geometric sans-serif (Inter, Geist)
Spacing: Structured grid
Images: Abstract, illustrations, screenshots
Borders: Slightly rounded (4-8px)
Overall: Professional but not boring
```text

### 3. Bold & Energetic (Startups, Creative)

```text
Colors: High contrast, neon accents
Typography: Bold weights, expressive
Spacing: Tighter, dynamic
Images: Photography with personality
Borders: Mix of sharp and rounded
Overall: Memorable, stands out
```text

### 4. Elegant & Premium (Luxury, High-end Services)

```text
Colors: Black, white, gold/muted accent
Typography: Serif headings, refined sans body
Spacing: Lots of white space
Images: High-quality, editorial style
Borders: Sharp or very subtle
Overall: Less is more, quality feel
```text

### 5. Warm & Friendly (Local Business, Personal Brand)

```text
Colors: Warm tones, earth colors
Typography: Friendly, approachable fonts
Spacing: Comfortable, not too formal
Images: Real people, behind-the-scenes
Borders: Rounded, soft
Overall: Like talking to a friend
```text

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Do This Instead |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| No defined direction | Inconsistent design | Document direction first |
| Following trends blindly | Doesn't fit brand | Match to audience expectations |
| Too many styles mixed | Chaotic, unprofessional | Pick one direction, commit |
| Ignoring competitors | May look out of place | Research industry norms |
| Over-designing | Distracts from content | When in doubt, simplify |

## Example

**Project:** Psychology practice website

```markdown
## Design Direction: Jacqui Howles

**Overall Feel:** Calm, professional, warm, trustworthy, approachable

**Visual Approach:**
- Minimal (not cluttered)
- Light (with warm accents)
- Soft (not harsh or clinical)
- Organic (human, not corporate)

**Reference Sites:**
1. therapydenmark.com - Like: calm colors, breathing room
2. headspace.com - Like: friendly illustrations, clear CTAs
3. betterhelp.com - Avoid: too corporate, stock photos

**Design Tokens:**

Typography:
- Headings: DM Sans or Nunito (rounded, friendly)
- Body: Inter or system fonts (readable)
- Feel: Soft, rounded, not clinical

Colors:
- Primary: Soft sage green (#7C9885) - calm, growth
- Secondary: Warm cream (#F5F1EB) - approachable
- Accent: Soft terracotta (#C4A484) - warmth
- Text: Charcoal (#2D3436) - readable, not harsh black

Spacing:
- Base: 8px
- Section padding: 80-120px
- Generous white space throughout

Borders:
- Radius: 12-16px (soft, rounded)
- Cards and buttons feel approachable

Shadows:
- Subtle, soft shadows only
- No harsh drop shadows

Images:
- Professional photo of Jacqui (warm, approachable)
- Abstract/nature imagery for backgrounds
- Avoid: stock photos, clinical settings
```text
